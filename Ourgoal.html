Hypersonic UAV Program

A fully autonomous aircraft designed to operate beyond supersonic flight, redefining speed, efficiency, and environmental impact.

Program Overview

The Aerius hypersonic UAV program is a long-term research and development effort aimed at creating an unmanned aerial platform capable of sustained supersonic and hypersonic flight. The aircraft is designed from the ground up to operate without onboard crew, enabling performance envelopes and mission profiles unattainable by conventional aviation.

Core Technology Pillars

Full Autonomy

Advanced flight control, navigation, and decision-making systems enable autonomous operation across the entire mission lifecycle, from launch to recovery.

Novel Propulsion

Research into unconventional propulsion architectures optimized for high-speed atmospheric flight, bridging the gap between turbojet, ramjet, and future hybrid systems.

Sonic Boom Mitigation

Aerodynamic shaping and trajectory optimization techniques aimed at reducing ground-level sonic boom impact, enabling more flexible operational envelopes.

Design Philosophy

Rather than adapting existing aircraft concepts, the platform is developed specifically for extreme-speed unmanned flight. Structural design, thermal management, aerodynamics, and control laws are co-designed to function as a single integrated system.

Potential Applications

Rapid Reconnaissance

Time-critical intelligence and surveillance missions.

Technology Demonstration

Validation of hypersonic flight and propulsion concepts.

Scientific Research

High-altitude and high-speed atmospheric experimentation.

Future Civil Concepts

Foundational research for low-boom high-speed aviation.

Program Status

The hypersonic UAV remains a research-driven initiative. Subscale testing, computational modeling, and propulsion experimentation are used to progressively validate key technologies before full-scale implementation.
